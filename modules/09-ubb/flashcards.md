# 🎴 Flashcards — Usage-Based Billing (UBB)

<details>
<summary><b>Q1: What is 1 AI Credit worth in USD?</b></summary>

**$0.01** — so a $10 budget = 1,000 credits.
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q2: Two Copilot things that are FREE and never consume credits?</b></summary>

**Code completion** and **Next Edit suggestions**.
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q3: Three budget layers where customers can cap overage?</b></summary>

**Enterprise · Organization · User** (plus cost centers for chargeback).
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q4: Customer says "I need to chargeback by team." Response?</b></summary>

"Cost centers govern overage ceilings and Azure sub separation at enterprise or cost-center level. Real chargeback via caps and reporting."
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q5: Customer says "this feels like vendor lock-in." Response?</b></summary>

"GitHub is built on choice — model choice, agent choice, experience choice. Devs pick IDE/CLI/editor. This aligns with the industry-standard billing model."
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q6: Customer says "this is more complex than what we have today." Response?</b></summary>

"Start simple: pooled entitlements + one enterprise cap. Add cost center / user controls only where you need them. Phased approach."
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q7: What changed with fallback experiences under UBB?</b></summary>

**No more zero-cost fallback.** All usage is governed by available credits and admin budget controls.
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Q8: 5 smart usage tips to share with customers?</b></summary>

1. Reduce context size
2. Scope prompts clearly
3. Default to Auto model, override only when needed
4. Monitor heavy users (agents + power users drive spikes)
5. Set user-level limits
</details>
